The Indian Army, established in 1895 and restructured post-independence in 1947, is the largest branch of the Indian Armed Forces, focused on land-based operations. It includes diverse units such as infantry, armored, artillery, and engineers, along with elite forces like the Parachute Regiment and Special Forces. Renowned for its high-altitude warfare expertise, the Army operates in various terrains and actively participates in UN peacekeeping missions. Headquartered in New Delhi, with General Manoj Pande as the current Chief of Army Staff (as of 2024), the Indian Army emphasizes modernization and comprehensive training through institutions like the Indian Military Academy in Dehradun and the Officers Training Academy in Chennai.

About Us

The Indian Army is the land-based branch and the largest component of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Army, and its professional head is the Chief of Army Staff, a four-star general.

Our Mission

Our mission is to ensure the defense of India and maintain the security of our borders. We are committed to protecting our nation and its citizens from external aggression and internal threats, as well as supporting peace and stability in the region.
