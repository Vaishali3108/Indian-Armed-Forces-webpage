The Indian Air Force (IAF), established in 1932, is the aerial defense arm of the Indian Armed Forces. It operates a diverse fleet including fighter jets like the Su-30MKI and MiG-29, transport aircraft such as the C-130J, and advanced helicopters like the Apache and Chinook. The IAF's roles encompass air superiority, strategic airlift, reconnaissance, and humanitarian missions. Headquartered in New Delhi, the IAF is led by the Chief of Air Staff, currently Air Chief Marshal Vivek Ram Chaudhari (as of 2024). It is known for its strategic operations, including the use of precision-guided munitions and participation in international exercises and peacekeeping missions. The Indian Air Force also focuses on modernization and enhancing its capabilities to address evolving security threats.

About Us

The Indian Air Force (IAF), established on October 8, 1932, is India's premier aerial defense force, known for its excellence and commitment to national security.

Our Mission

Our mission is to defend India's airspace, maintain peace, enhance our capabilities, foster excellence, and support national development. We strive to protect the nation's sovereignty and contribute to global stability through advanced technology and dedicated service.
