The Indian Navy, established in 1612 and officially named post-independence in 1950, serves as the maritime branch of the Indian Armed Forces, tasked with protecting India's maritime interests and borders. It operates a diverse fleet, including aircraft carriers like INS Vikramaditya and submarines like INS Arihant, supported by various aircraft such as the MiG-29K and P-8I. With approximately 67,000 personnel, the Navy conducts operations ranging from humanitarian aid to anti-piracy, and engages in international exercises like MALABAR and VARUNA to enhance global maritime security. Headquartered in New Delhi, the Indian Navy also runs the premier training institute, the Indian Naval Academy in Kerala.

About Us

The Indian Navy is the naval branch of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Navy. The Chief of Naval Staff, a four-star admiral, commands the navy.

Our Mission

Our mission is to protect India's maritime interests and ensure the security of our nation's coastal borders. We are committed to the defense of our country and to promoting peace and stability in the Indian Ocean region.
